Gwen Boon

Owner of Klijs & Boon on the Voorstraat, Utrecht

"We want women to be feminine but a little bit tough, sexy and more confident"

Walking into Klijs & Boon on a Wednesday before opening time it is immediately clear we are at the trendy centre of Voorstraat, with coffee drinkers basking in the morning sun next door and passers-by eager to browse the boutique fashion stores either side. Klijs & Boon is one of the most popular fashion stops in the area and the bright, welcoming décor is complimented perfectly by Gwen herself who now invites me for a coffee and chat.

What was the inspiration for Klijs & Boon?

"I studied Art and Economics at the Art Academy in Utrecht and then went into high fashion at a brand in Antwerp but I knew becoming a designer wasn't for me so I returned to Utrecht.

"When I returned from Antwerp I looked for what was missing in Utrecht and started the shop with a friend with the idea of selling high fashion at an affordable price. I don't want to be a boutique store where only a few people come in. It’s a fun place where people can be themselves so it’s more personal and aren’t terrified of the prices. When people visit the shop we don't even talk about clothes usually.

Can you tell me about the fashion scene here and how you fit in?

"I enjoy the fashion scene here because it is more personal and collaborative and doesn't have the same attitude of the high fashion world. Although in Holland there is still a lot of pretending, and then in Copenhagen, where I always go, its so nice, I know people really well, its a lot of fun and I don't need to wear heels to be fashionable.

"I remember once going to a fashion show in Amsterdam and I was wearing a really cool suit with all sorts of colours and sneakers, but all the other girls were wearing the same typical Amsterdam dresses so it was really awkward when we were photographed together!

"We want women to be feminine but a little bit tough, sexy and more confident. For me sexy might mean having baggy top and shorter bottoms, maybe a top that’s a little see-through if you have a nice bra underneath. I like a good mixture that’s also classy.

"We try to get to know our brands, we did a really personal interview with one of our biggest brands brands libertine libertine about their production and why they do it. I wanted to have fashion labels that have been out there for a while and have good production because you know the quality will be better. We try to balance the commercial items by always offering something new as well. We dont try to push our own tastes on visitors all the time but I like to suggest things when im enthusiastic about a piece, which tend to be the thins in the window.

What do you enjoy outside the fashion world?

"After High school I travelled Australia; surfing half a year, it’s always a great experience when you're young and so free but it’s always just a dream.

"People told this girl who had been backpacking for five years that she was trying to escape from real life but she said no, it’s just that she can. That made me feel worried that I now have responsibility because I’ve always been a short term girl but at the same time I love my shop and always want to be involved. So I’m always interested in new projects and bringing people together, we just brought out a new water bottle which I really want to bring to scale.
