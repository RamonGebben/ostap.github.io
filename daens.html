Willem van Oostrum

Owner of Daen's on Korte Minrebroedersstraat, Utrecht

"I really like to see people come into the shop wearing something we used to sell 6 years ago. I think sustainable and hardy fashion is very important; sometimes it’s worth paying extra for something which will stay with you"

Its a sunny day in June, after having a browse through the fashion collection and a peek at the solitary hotel room around the side, Willem invites me to join him on Daen's cafe terrace which is always buzzing during these months. Daen's seems to be one of those locations in Utrecht that makes you feel like your in a much larger city than you really are, and its perfect for watching passers-by or exhibitions going on in the square.

Can you tell me a little bit about yourself and how Daen's came about and future plans?

“I went to the Art Academy in Arnhem for product design but after a while I knew design wasn’t for me and I’d rather sell it. Then eventually I opened the first Daen's store on the Shoutenstraat opposite where Bigoli is now in 1969. I sold classical menswear such as suits, ties, jackets. Maybe back then society was more classical; people are now going to work in smart casual wear. Soon after, I rented the next door space to start a women’s clothing line with Ilse (now at P.I.L, still on the Shoutenstraat).

“There was originally a fire department where Daen's is now; it still says 'Brandweer' on the outside of our building. Daen's was originally a clothes store, but I wanted to open up a food and wine bar the way you have them in Paris where people can stop by after a long day’s work or shopping and then go off again.

“We now also have a place for home-made breakfast, lunch and snacks without anything artificial

“I like gardening a lot, one of the biggest dreams I have is to build a huge greenhouse on a remote piece of land set up with lots of example balconies and roof terraces. People could just relax and get inspired.

It's an interesting combination of things going on here, can you tell me a bit more about what you do?

“Yes, some people advise me to turn the whole place into a cafe but for me it’s the combination of fashion, cafe, wine bar and hotel that makes Daen's special.

We have a mix of fashion brands from France, Italy and Scandinavia. I really like to see people come into the shop wearing something we used to sell 6 years ago. I think sustainable and hardy fashion is very important; sometimes it’s worth paying extra for something which will stay with you. A guy came into the shop recently looking for a new pair of jeans because his last pair was worn out at the groin from his bike; I told him if he really likes that previous pair we would repair it for him.

“We like to make sure our clothes are timeless but also still fashionable to help people over that threshold, otherwise people will always be wearing the same thing. When you buy on the internet you don’t get the impression you need of the quality and you find people always buy similar things, we try to add something new but without its going out of fashion tomorrow.

I see that this is a very popular stop for wine after work or shopping, what's your favourite during these summer months?

"We get our wine from a great little wine shop around the corner called Margaret Wines; they are really good quality wines and are very specialist which helps us stay off the beaten track with wines.

“My favourite for the summer is a good Pinot Noir which is a little chilled and light but still full-bodied. We know that buying wine in a supermarket is usually a bit of a lottery so we try to keep the selection small and quality high, a lot of it is down to trust.
