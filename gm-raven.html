Guus van der Kemp

Owner of GMraven on the Shoutenstraat, Utrecht

"Do this for me and ill return the favour; if you work together in that way then you start coming out with cool things"

I have been down here recently to chat with Skip, so this time I catch Guus after his sailing holiday to quiz him on his cool custom suit concept to find out where it started. These days are very exciting for Guus as his ideas are gaining great traction but he needs investment to take his dream to the next level. We perch on the shop window at street level while the rain begins.

What does GMraven do?

"We make suits and other things like coats, but we work with local artists to put their work in the clothing. Just recently we opened up shop here and were always looking for more artists who would like to work with us.

So tell us about yourself and how this all came about

"I studied international business management at the University of Amsterdam and then interned with Ernst and Young accountancy. It was awesome but it opened my eyes to the fact I didn't want to go into finance. I had to advise a company, I said you have to drop that person, and that person but then it actually happened. It didn't feel to good so I decided I had to do something for myself.

"I went to Shanghai. I wanted to learn mandarin but it was particularly difficult to get a job for a foreigner because it was during the financial crisis. I lasted only a few months but I met a lot of people in the fashion industry by chance and thats where I came up with the concept for GM Raven. I set up my tailors and network in China and went back to Holland for investment.

Where does your love of art come from?

"My family loves art so there was always a lot of influence from when I was growing up. I appreciate so many kinds of art, but I really like bright and vibrant pop art especially. There are lots of options here so people can have their favorite art inside their suits no matter what the style.

How does it work with the artists?

"At first it was very hard to find artists but now they see what I do and want to get involved. So its a mutually beneficial arrangement where they provide amazing art work to us while we provide a great platform for their work that they can show their friends.!

"I think it's the future, working with a community and searching for collaborations to produce something new. It used to be that you pay me 1000 euros for this and I pay 1000 euros for that, but now its just, you do this for me and ill return the favour. If you work together in that way then you start coming out with cool things until you have a business.

And your even sharing your shop with Don't Skip Coffee..

"Its another cool collaboration. Its great that my clients can come down and have a delicious coffee, so they are customers are of both the suits and coffee. Skip is open at the weekends so its the perfect opportunity to check out the suits.

So you had your opening recently, how did it go?

"Yeh, its the mission of GMraven to host four galleries a year. So we had a gallery down the road during the opening where we hosted around 100 people who got see the beautiful artwork on the wall with the same art printed within the suits.

The new place needs to be small friendly and somewhere that's a little bit difficult to find. Hopefully i'll be able to take some holiday though because I only just had my first break after three years to go sailing in the north of Holland. I usually try to get away every now and again to a lake about an hour north of here to go on a day's sailing.

"In the future I hope to bring the manufacturing to Holland and set up a shop in Shanghai because the Chinese love European design. I've always lived in Utrecht and it's great to see Utrecht becoming more known around the world so it's a great place for me to start.
