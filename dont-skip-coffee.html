Skip Roeland

Owner of Don't Skip Coffee on the Shoutenstraat, Utrecht

"I was drinking litres at a time and thinking ’maybe ill have this, and this and this!’, by the end of the day my heart was pounding"

We drop down into the cool cellar of Shoutenstraat and Skip immediately begins preparing coffee for us before we gradually settle on the large table in the next room. Skip shares this space with Guus of GM Raven, a creative suit maker, which makes for a fun atmosphere with the guys clearly in their element.

So what are we drinking now?

"Were drinking an afternoon organic coffee, a nice blend of Indonesian and Ethiopian which is earthy with red fruit. The Indonesian reduces acidity while adding a herbal edge, tobacco, wood.

What tips can you give anyone thats looking to be more adventurous with their coffee?

"Starbucks use good beans but roast too much, it removes flavour. There could have been fruity and acidic tones, but instead it turns burnt and ashy. I once had a machine from Starbucks, when I opened it the grinder was covered in a greasy peanut butter substance. The beans were not dry enough so there was too much oil and with oxygen it gives a metallic flavour.

"why not try a cold brew coffee, the tones are far less acidic we noticed when you let it sit for for about 12 hours at around 7 degrees you have a beautiful coffee that is thick and vicious. Just use the sieve and put it through the aero-press, filtering once or twice and the result is a really nice clean, thick coffee. Something very easy to drink in the summer but just watch out for the shakes because it can be a little too easy to drink!

So how did you discover your passion for coffee?

“My father started a brewery 20 years ago for special beers but a few years ago I decided to let my brothers take over the company without me and I went to berlin.

“I took on an assignment with Bonanza coffee heroes Berlin, where I began to work there for 3 months which turned into a year working in the bar. I went there thinking I knew something about coffee but then they showed me the ways you can roast; the ways you can grind. There was so much variety that I knew nothing about. It ignited my coffee passion not just for drinking it but making it and changing it every time, every brew you can make something else, I really love that part.

"it was amazing, I was drinking litres at a time and thinking ’maybe ill have this, and this and this!’, by the end of the day my heart was pounding and instead thought "this is not ok, this is not ok!" and eventually you learn

"Berlin has a great coffee scene, baristas are more open to sharing information than in Amsterdam, where if they dont know what I do then they just say "here’s your coffee, sit over there". I try to be open like in Berlin and in a language that people understand

What typically happens here in the cellar?

"During the week I am roasting, delivering coffee to customers and trying new ways to brew while people come to see the suits. In the weekends people come to taste the coffee.

"If people don’t know how to make good coffee I want them to come by and we will work it out together. I want people to throw out their Senseo and choose filter coffee. People come here and ask for my help, I want this project to be that beacon of information.

You share the space with GM Raven, how did that come about?

"Guus (GM Raven) was my old house mate and he really sold the unique concept to me. Guus found this place and we both wanted to try combining our passions to create something unique.

"Things are always changing in here and everything is for sale including this table and bench from Basematters. All together we have a combination of designer suits, coffee, furniture and art. It’s the new economy where people help each other and eventually you will be better off.

"Sometimes Yolanda, a fashion stylist from Public Image Labels (P.I.L.) upstairs shares this space for her own workshops while I’ll be the personal barista, it works out great!
